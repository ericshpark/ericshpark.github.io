Module 8 · Visual Identity

The Logo

A heraldic crown that doubles as a skyline. A wordmark that doubles as a thesis.

The mark is a five-point heraldic crown set in navy and gold, with the lowercase wordmark ericshpark beneath. Read denotatively, it is a crown. Read connotatively, the rising vertical bars also read as a city skyline or a bar chart, placing academic responsibility (the crown) and quantitative discipline (the bars) in the same shape. The tagline Rigor · Responsibility · Craft fixes the meaning, in the way Stuart Hall describes an anchor fixing the meaning of an image.

Three sizes

Per the Module 8 brief: three sizes, with the middle size in black & white. All three are vector SVGs, so they remain crisp at any scale; PNG exports are provided for printing or upload.

The AI prompts

Per the Module 8 logo assignment requirement "show prompts and what AI you used." Tool: Anthropic Claude (Opus 4.6), used for both concept iteration and final SVG generation.

Prompt 1 · Concept Propose three logo directions for a personal brand ericshpark that fuses a heraldic crown with a tech / quant-finance aesthetic. The crown should visually double as either a city skyline or a bar chart. Lowercase wordmark beneath. Give me a clear color palette for each direction.
Prompt 2 · Silhouette Build the crown as five vertical rising points on a rectangular band. Center point tallest, two flanking inner points medium height, two outer points lowest. Strictly symmetrical. Geometric, not curvy, so the rising bars also read as a bar chart. Render as SVG with a clean path.
Prompt 3 · Ornament Add small ornament: a gold diamond floating above the tallest point, gold circles atop the inner points and (smaller) atop the outer points. Three dark pearls inset into the gold band. Stroke everything in navy at 2px so the mark holds at small sizes.
Prompt 4 · Wordmark Set the wordmark ericshpark in a bold geometric sans-serif (Inter or Manrope, weight 700, slight negative letter-spacing) directly under the crown. Add a thin gold rule and a small all-caps tagline RIGOR · RESPONSIBILITY · CRAFT with wide letter-spacing for gravitas.
Prompt 5 · Variants Produce three variants of the mark:
1. Large, full color (wordmark + crown + tagline).
2. Medium, pure black-and-white. The band becomes a white panel outlined in black, the jewels become outlined shapes, and the mark must remain legible.
3. Small, icon-only on a navy circular badge, suitable as a favicon or profile picture, with the crown in white plus gold accents.

Why this mark passes the Module 8 criteria

Clarity at scale. The path is built from straight verticals and miter joins, so the silhouette survives downsampling to a 16×16 favicon without becoming muddy.
Color and B&W both work. The composition relies on shape, not on the gold accent. Strip the color and a legible crown remains.
Denotation and connotation. Denotatively a five-point heraldic crown. Connotatively a city skyline plus a bar chart, in an academic-quant palette, with a tagline that fixes the meaning.
